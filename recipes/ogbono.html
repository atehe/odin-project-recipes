Ogbono Soup

Description

Ogbono soup is a delectable Nigerian concoction soup and it’s super easy to prepare. It’s a thick and hearty stew made with Ogbono seeds (wild mango seeds) loaded with meats and fish simmered in a light delicious broth.

When Ogbono is cooked it has a mucilaginous (slimy) texture like cooked Okra and Jute leaves (Ewedu). This is a stew and not a soup in the real sense but we call it soup anyways!

Ingredients

  • Ogbono Seed
  • Cube Seasoning
  • Palm Oil
  • Scent leave
  • Pepper
  • Pomo
  • Meat

Steps

  1. Grind your ogbono with crayfish and set aside, turning the flame put your pot add your palm oil when hot put in your diced onions fry for a while let say have way, diced into two pour the first one in the grinded ogbono and set aside.
  2. Now add all your cooked meat, fish,pomo, chicken, and stock fish mix for a while then add in your water allow it to boil for a very long time then add in your seasoning cube, ginger and garlic, locust bean, shrimps and allow to cook too.
  3. Now pour in your ogbono and cook too then check for your seasoning cubes and others
  4. Add in your scent leave and allow to simmer for a while and its done
